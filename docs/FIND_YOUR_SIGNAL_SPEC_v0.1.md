# FIND YOUR SIGNAL — Product Spec v0.1

**Status:** Approved concept / implementation-ready discovery
**Project:** SPACEFROGS Kickstarter PWA
**Goal:** Turn a backer's answers into a meaningful SPACEFROGS Signal Profile without creating an expensive fully custom character pipeline.

## Product Principle

> You do not choose your Spacefrog. The Signal reveals what you already carry.

This is not a novelty quiz and not a promise of a fully bespoke miniature. It is a canon-grounded Signal Classification Experience.

## Experience Tiers

### 1. Discover Your Signal
Included for all visitors/backers.

Outputs:
- Signal Type
- Primary Gate
- Secondary Trait
- Squad Affinity
- Visual Strain
- Signal color
- Gate symbol
- Shareable result card

### 2. Join the Archive
Backer reward or account feature.

Adds:
- Callsign
- Persistent Signal ID
- Digital Archive certificate
- Backer badge
- Entry in a curated WIT Archive ledger
- Downloadable result card/PDF

### 3. Become a Spacefrog — Founder Edition
Strictly limited premium reward.

Uses a curated modular system rather than unrestricted customization:
- approved body archetype
- approved armor modules
- approved Signal palette
- approved Gate symbol
- approved Aura Nova variant
- one curated portrait/render

No promise of exact facial likeness, unlimited revisions, or unique production tooling per backer.

## Quiz Design

Use 9–12 scenario questions. Avoid shallow questions such as favorite color or self-rated bravery.

Each answer contributes weighted scores to the Nine Signal tendencies:
- WIT — pattern / guidance
- KRASH — action / breach
- FLUX — connection / systems
- LUXA — protection / life
- ROKK — stability / endurance
- SHADE — observation / precision
- MIRE — intuition / deep listening
- JUKE — movement / improvisation
- FREDDIE — unity / morale

## Example Questions

### Q1 — The Closing Gate
A teammate is trapped and the route closes in twenty seconds. What do you do first?

- Map the sequence and assign exact actions. → WIT
- Break the obstruction before it seals. → KRASH
- Reroute the mechanism powering the gate. → FLUX
- Reach the trapped teammate and stabilize them. → LUXA
- Hold the route open for everyone else. → ROKK
- Remove the threat watching the corridor. → SHADE
- Listen for the route the system is about to reveal. → MIRE
- Find a route nobody else has noticed. → JUKE
- Keep the team coordinated and moving together. → FREDDIE

### Q2 — The Failed Plan
The original plan is gone. Which instinct arrives first?

- Rebuild the pattern from what remains. → WIT
- Create momentum before fear takes over. → KRASH
- Reconnect the resources still available. → FLUX
- Check who is most at risk. → LUXA
- Establish one position that will hold. → ROKK
- Become still and observe what changed. → SHADE
- Listen for the consequence hidden in the noise. → MIRE
- Move before the situation closes around you. → JUKE
- Give everyone one clear rhythm to follow. → FREDDIE

### Q3 — What Must Not Be Lost?
Choose the one thing you protect first:

- Direction
- Courage
- Connection
- Life
- Ground
- Clarity
- Meaning
- Freedom of movement
- Each other

## Result Logic

### Primary Signal
Highest weighted score.

### Secondary Signal
Second-highest score, unless too close to the primary; in that case classify as a hybrid.

### Primary Gate
Derived from the dominant decision pattern, not chosen directly.

Suggested initial gates:
- Pattern
- Force
- Connection
- Life
- Foundation
- Silence
- Echo
- Motion
- Unity

### Visual Strain
One of the approved world manuals:
- First Signal
- Bioforge
- Nine Gates
- Denied / Tri-Six
- Black Neon / Old Gods

Initially, assign strain through a short second-stage preference choice after the psychological result. This keeps personality classification separate from aesthetics.

## Cost-Control Rules

- No unlimited custom art.
- No unique miniature sculpt for every user.
- No promise of exact likeness.
- Use a finite modular asset library.
- Premium outputs are curated, not user-directed without limits.
- Limit Founder Edition quantity before campaign launch.
- Every deliverable must be automatable or reviewable in batches.

## Anti-Cringe Rules

- Never call it “Which frog are you?”
- Never use childish personality labels.
- Never assign diagnoses.
- Never claim scientific psychological accuracy.
- Never reduce users to one heroic adjective.
- Keep result writing serious, cinematic and humane.
- Make every profile contain a strength, a cost and a relational role.

## MVP Scope

Version 0.1 should include:
- landing screen
- 9 scenario questions
- weighted scoring
- primary + secondary Signal result
- squad affinity
- one of two visual strains at launch: First Signal or Bioforge
- shareable result card
- local save or account-linked save
- analytics for completion, drop-off and share rate

## Success Metrics

- Quiz completion rate
- Result share rate
- Kickstarter click-through after result
- Backer conversion after result
- Most/least selected answers
- Distribution across the Nine
- Percentage choosing Join the Archive
- Support requests per 1,000 completions

## Recommended Build Order

1. Lock the nine scoring profiles.
2. Write 12 scenario questions.
3. Define result naming system.
4. Design one result card template.
5. Build scoring prototype.
6. Test with 15–30 people blind.
7. Check whether results feel accurate without explanation.
8. Add account persistence and Kickstarter CTA.
9. Add Join the Archive reward layer.
10. Consider Founder Edition only after operational costing.

## Canon Rule

The experience reveals a user's Signal affinity. It does not make them one of the canonical Squad Nine and does not alter established character canon.
